# Human Reproductive System

## Male Reproductive System

### Primary Organs
**Testes** (singular: testis)
- Produce sperm (spermatogenesis)
- Produce testosterone
- Located in scrotum (temperature regulation)

### Accessory Structures
- **Epididymis**: Sperm maturation and storage
- **Vas deferens**: Sperm transport tube
- **Seminal vesicles**: Produce seminal fluid (60% of semen)
- **Prostate gland**: Produces alkaline fluid (30% of semen)
- **Bulbourethral glands**: Produce pre-ejaculate fluid
- **Penis**: Copulatory organ
- **Urethra**: Passage for semen and urine

### Sperm Structure
- Head: Contains nucleus and acrosome (enzymes)
- Midpiece: Mitochondria for energy
- Tail (flagellum): Locomotion

## Female Reproductive System

### Primary Organs
**Ovaries** (2)
- Produce eggs (oogenesis)
- Produce estrogen and progesterone
- Release one egg per month (ovulation)

### Accessory Structures
- **Fallopian tubes (oviducts)**: Egg transport, fertilization site
- **Uterus**: Implantation and fetal development
  - Endometrium: Inner lining (sheds during menstruation)
  - Myometrium: Muscular layer
- **Cervix**: Opening to uterus
- **Vagina**: Birth canal, copulatory organ

## Menstrual Cycle (~28 days)

### Phase 1: Menstruation (Days 1-5)
- Endometrium sheds
- Low hormone levels

### Phase 2: Follicular Phase (Days 1-14)
- FSH stimulates follicle development
- Estrogen levels rise
- Endometrium thickens

### Phase 3: Ovulation (Day 14)
- LH surge triggers egg release
- Egg enters fallopian tube

### Phase 4: Luteal Phase (Days 15-28)
- Corpus luteum forms
- Produces progesterone
- Maintains endometrium
- If no fertilization: corpus luteum degenerates → menstruation

## Hormones

### Male
- **Testosterone**: Sperm production, secondary sex characteristics
- **FSH**: Stimulates spermatogenesis
- **LH**: Stimulates testosterone production

### Female
- **Estrogen**: Endometrium growth, secondary sex characteristics
- **Progesterone**: Maintains endometrium
- **FSH**: Follicle development
- **LH**: Triggers ovulation

## Fertilization
- Occurs in fallopian tube
- Sperm penetrates egg
- Zygote forms (diploid)
- Travels to uterus for implantation (6-7 days)
