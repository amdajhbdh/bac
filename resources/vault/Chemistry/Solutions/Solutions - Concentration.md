# Solution Concentration

## Definitions

**Solution** = Solute + Solvent
- Solute: Substance being dissolved
- Solvent: Substance doing the dissolving

## Concentration Units

### 1. Molarity (M)
Moles of solute per liter of solution

**Formula**: M = n/V = moles/liters

Units: mol/L or M

**Example**: 2 moles NaCl in 1 L water = 2 M

### 2. Molality (m)
Moles of solute per kilogram of solvent

**Formula**: m = n/kg_solvent

Units: mol/kg

### 3. Mass Percent
**Formula**: % = (mass_solute/mass_solution) × 100%

**Example**: 10 g salt in 90 g water
% = 10/100 × 100% = 10%

### 4. Volume Percent
**Formula**: % = (V_solute/V_solution) × 100%

### 5. Parts Per Million (ppm)
**Formula**: ppm = (mass_solute/mass_solution) × 10^6

Used for very dilute solutions

### 6. Mole Fraction (χ)
**Formula**: χ_A = n_A/(n_A + n_B + ...)

Sum of all mole fractions = 1

## Dilution Formula
M_1V_1 = M_2V_2

Where:
- M_1 = initial molarity
- V_1 = initial volume
- M_2 = final molarity
- V_2 = final volume

## Conversions

### Mass to Moles
n = m/M (M = molar mass)

### Molarity to Molality
Need density of solution

## Examples

**Example 1**: Make 0.5 M NaCl, 2 L
n = M×V = 0.5×2 = 1 mol
mass = 1×58.5 = 58.5 g NaCl needed

**Example 2**: Dilute 100 mL of 6 M HCl to 2 M
M_1V_1 = M_2V_2
6×100 = 2×V_2
V_2 = 300 mL (add 200 mL water)
