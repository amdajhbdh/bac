## STUDY TECHNIQUES

### The 50-10 Rule
- Study for 50 minutes
- Break for 10 minutes
- Repeat

### Active Learning Checklist
For EVERY topic:
- [ ] Read concept notes
- [ ] Explain out loud (Feynman technique)
- [ ] Do ALL easy practice problems
- [ ] Do at least 10 medium problems
- [ ] Create at least 1 diagram
- [ ] Watch at least 1 video
- [ ] Link concepts to other topics

### Problem-Solving Strategy
1. **Read** the problem carefully
2. **Draw** a diagram (always!)
3. **Identify** what you know and what you need
4. **Solve** step by step
5. **Check** your answer
6. **Understand** why it works

## PRO TIPS

### Speed Up Learning
1. **Don't just read** - solve problems immediately
2. **Teach out loud** - explain to an imaginary student
3. **Draw everything** - visual memory is powerful
4. **Link concepts** - connect math to physics to chemistry
5. **Use real examples** - relate to everyday life

### When Stuck
1. Check [[Resource Links]] for videos
2. Use Wolfram Alpha to check answers
3. Draw a diagram
4. Take a 5-minute break
5. Come back and try again

### Maximize Retention
1. **Spaced repetition**: Review after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days
2. **Active recall**: Close notes and explain from memory
3. **Interleaving**: Mix different topics
4. **Sleep**: Get 7-8 hours each night

---

## 🚨 TROUBLESHOOTING

### "I don't understand a concept"
1. Watch the video linked in the note
2. Read the "Related Notes" section
3. Try the easy practice problems
4. Use external tools (Desmos, PhET, etc.)
5. Move on and come back later

### "Practice problems are too hard"
1. Review the concept notes again
2. Watch a video explanation
3. Do easier problems first
4. Check the answer and work backwards
5. Ask for help (online forums)

### "I'm running out of time"
1. Focus on easy and medium problems
2. Skip hard problems for now
3. Prioritize topics you struggle with most
4. Use videos for quick understanding
5. Create summary sheets instead of detailed notes
